Whispers of Fern

A poem about finding peace in the forest's quiet corners.

In shades of green, the old world sleeps,
Where sunlight dapples, secrets keeps.
A silent root, a twisting vine,
In ancient patterns, so divine.

The moss-clad stone, a soft embrace,
A whisper from this quiet place.
No hurried steps, no rushing sound,
Just peace upon this hallowed ground.

A fragile whisper, soft and low,
In places where the wild things grow.
The fern unfurls, a gentle hand,
A silent story in the land.

The Beekeeper's Tale

A story of honey, hives, and summer days.

The golden sun, a patient light,
Draws nectar from the morning's flight.
In quiet fields, a buzzing hum,
A whispered promise, work to come.

The scent of clover, sweet and deep,
While tiny artisans awake from sleep.
They dance on petals, soft and wide,
With golden dust, they fly inside.

The beekeeper, a gentle hand,
Walks softly on this sun-drenched land.
He learns the rhythm of the hive,
A world where humble life can thrive.

And when the evening shadows fall,
He hears the sweetness in their call.
A sweet reward, a gift so pure,
A taste of sunlight to endure.

Garden's End

A reflective poem on the passing of seasons.

The crimson leaves begin to fall,
An amber hue across the wall.
The weary blooms, a final sigh,
As summer's vibrant moments die.

The fading light, a gentle plea,
To rest beneath the willow tree.
The hummingbirds have flown away,
To find a warmer, brighter day.

And though the air grows crisp and cold,
A different beauty will unfold.
The garden sleeps, a silent promise,
For gentle green to rise from this.
